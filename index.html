The Problem

Most growing companies hit a wall between $2M and $15M — and never see it coming.

01
Managing by instinct, not intelligence

Your bookkeeper closes the books monthly but nobody's translating those numbers into forward-looking decisions. You're reacting to the past, not steering toward the future.

02
Capital events expose the gaps

Whether it's a bank line, a fundraise, or a strategic buyer — sophisticated capital asks questions your QuickBooks dashboard can't answer. The absence of infrastructure becomes visible at exactly the wrong moment.

03
Growth is outpacing the systems

You've scaled past what a bookkeeper can handle but a full-time C-Suite hire isn't justified yet. The in-between is where most companies operate for years — and where the most value is lost.
